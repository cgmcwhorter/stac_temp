% Quiz 1
\quizsol \textit{True/False}: The number 1 is prime. \pspace

\sol The statement is \textit{false}. A prime number is an integer greater than 1 that can only be factored as the product of one and itself. So for example, the integer 11 is prime because we can only factor 11 as $11= 1 \cdot 11$. However, the integer 12 is not prime because we can write $12= 2 \cdot 6$, neither of which are 1 or 12.
